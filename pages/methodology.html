Methodology & Technical Details

Understanding the data sources, modeling approaches, and estimation methods behind the National Energy Affordability Tool.

Methodology Overview

The National Energy Affordability Tool estimates household-level energy costs, consumption, and emissions for all occupied housing units in the United States. Key methodological components include:

  • Household Simulation: Statistical matching of survey data to create representative households
  • Energy Modeling: End-use based estimation of electricity and fuel consumption
  • Cost Estimation: Application of local utility rates and fuel prices
  • Emissions Calculation: Grid emission factors and fuel combustion emissions

Primary Data Sources

Our methodology integrates multiple authoritative data sources to achieve comprehensive coverage:

Household Characteristics

  • American Community Survey (ACS) 2017-2021: Demographics, income, housing characteristics
  • American Housing Survey (AHS): Housing unit details and energy equipment

Energy Data

  • EIA Residential Energy Consumption Survey (RECS): End-use consumption patterns
  • EIA Form 861: Utility service territories and rates
  • EIA Form 176: Natural gas consumption data

Climate Data

  • NOAA: Heating and cooling degree days by location
