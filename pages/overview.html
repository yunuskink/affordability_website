Energy Affordability Overview

A snapshot of how energy costs, burdens, and emissions shape affordability — and where relief and action matter most.

Energy costs touch every household, but the burden is not shared equally. Scroll to see how costs, budgets, and emissions stack up — and why targeted solutions matter.

01 • Energy costs

What households pay to stay powered

American households spend billions each year on electricity and fuels to heat, cool, and power their homes. High-cost end uses like space conditioning and water heating drive a large share of the total.

  • Low-income households carry a disproportionate share of the bill.
  • Cost spikes hit renters and residents of older homes hardest.
  • Reliable local data is critical for directing relief.
02 • Budget impacts

When bills squeeze household budgets

Energy burden measures the percent of income spent on energy. Crossing the 6% line is where households start making tradeoffs between essentials.

  • Many low-income families exceed the 6% affordability threshold.
  • Even modest efficiency upgrades can lower the burden quickly.
  • The affordability gap shows how much support is needed to close the distance.
Dig deeper: Explore burden distributions and gaps by community in the interactive tool.
03 • Emissions

Household energy and climate

Residential energy use is a significant slice of U.S. greenhouse gas emissions. Cutting bills and cutting carbon often move in the same direction.

  • Electricity and direct fuel use both contribute to household emissions.
  • Low-income households emit less per home but face higher burdens per dollar.
  • Targeted electrification and efficiency can lower both bills and emissions.
04 • Why this matters

Affordability and equity go together

Energy insecurity is a health, safety, and climate justice issue. The right data helps direct resources where they do the most good.

  • Assistance programs can target the highest-burden households.
  • Weatherization and electrification lower long-term bills.
  • Local insights help design equitable climate and energy policies.
